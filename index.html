What We Did & Why

A joint-embedding predictive architecture (JEPA) learns rich internal representations — we ask: how well do those representations predict human brain activity?

🎥

V-JEPA2 — Video Model

Facebook's video JEPA (ViT-L backbone). We extract features at different temporal sampling fractions (1%–10%) to find the optimal temporal coverage per TR.

📷

I-JEPA — Image Model

Facebook's image JEPA (ViT-H, ImageNet-22k). We aggregate across frame counts (8–240 frames/clip) to study how temporal pooling affects brain alignment.

🧠

fMRI Neural Encoding

Ridge regression maps model embeddings → Schaefer-1000 fMRI parcels. Lag-aligned to the hemodynamic response (3 TRs). Evaluated by Pearson correlation.

📊

Layer & Config Sweep

We compare 4 feature extraction points within V-JEPA (enc-10, enc-18, enc-20, enc-last-ln) and 5 temporal configs for each model type.

Processing Pipeline

End-to-end from raw video to brain prediction, with GPU optimization throughout.

STEP 01
Video Chunking
8s chunks, 6s pre-stimulus context, TR-aligned at 1.49s intervals
STEP 02
Frame Sampling
Uniform subsampling at configurable fraction (1–10%) or frame count (8–240)
STEP 03
JEPA Embedding
Multi-GPU bfloat16 inference; hook-based intermediate layer extraction
STEP 04
Ridge Regression
Dual-form kernel ridge (avoids D≫N OOM); α ∈ {0.1…1000}, 5-fold CV
STEP 05
Evaluation
Pearson r per parcel; mean, median, max, % positive; vs. ISC noise ceiling

Key Findings

What we learned from the sweep.

FINDING 01

V-JEPA outperforms I-JEPA

Video-native features from V-JEPA2 consistently yield higher mean Pearson r than image-based I-JEPA features (0.12–0.14 vs 0.08–0.09), suggesting temporal continuity is important for brain encoding during movie viewing.

FINDING 02

Less sampling can be better (V-JEPA)

1–2% temporal sampling outperforms 10%. Sparse uniform sampling reduces temporal redundancy, forcing the model to represent maximally diverse moments — this appears to align better with the brain's efficient coding of scene structure.

FINDING 03

More frames helps I-JEPA (up to 32)

I-JEPA performance increases from 8→32 frames and plateaus. An image model needs sufficient temporal pooling to capture motion and scene transitions it cannot natively model. Beyond 32f, averaging dilutes relevant signal.

FINDING 04

Final layer norm is most brain-like

enc-last-ln consistently outperforms earlier layers (enc-10, enc-18, enc-20). The most abstract, post-normalization representation is the best predictor of cortical fMRI activity across all 4 subjects.

FINDING 05

Best config matches noise ceiling

V-JEPA 2%, sub-02 achieves Pearson r = 0.1636, which exceeds the ISC noise ceiling of 0.1483. This suggests the model captures genuine signal, not just shared variance — strong evidence of brain–model alignment.

FINDING 06

Subject variability is real

Sub-01 and Sub-03 consistently outperform Sub-02 and Sub-05. This likely reflects both individual neural variability and signal quality differences in the fMRI recordings rather than model issues.

Technical Details

Implementation specifics for reproducibility.

Models

  • V-JEPA2: facebook/vjepa2-vitl-fpc64-256
  • I-JEPA: facebook/ijepa_vith14_22k
  • Hook-based intermediate layer extraction
  • bfloat16 inference, float16 storage
  • Multi-GPU with OOM retry schedule
VJEPA layers tapped: enc-10layer-fc2 (layer 10) enc-18layer-norm2 (layer 18) enc-20layer-fc2 (layer 20) enc-last-ln (final norm) ← best

Temporal Alignment

  • TR = 1.49 seconds
  • Chunk length: 8.0s, 6.0s pre-stimulus context
  • 3 TR hemodynamic lag correction
  • Clip-matched (not random) train/test split
  • 80% train / 20% test (58 test clips)
chunk_length = 8.0s seconds_before = 6.0s hrf_lag = 3 TRs (~4.5s) train_fraction = 0.80

Ridge Regression

  • Dual-form kernel ridge (solves N×N not D×D)
  • Avoids OOM when feature dim D ≫ N samples
  • Alpha: [0.1, 1.0, 10.0, 100.0, 1000.0]
  • Best alpha consistently 1000.0 (heavy L2)
  • Per-parcel prediction (1000 outputs)

Noise Ceiling

  • ISC (Inter-Subject Correlation) as proxy ceiling
  • 287 clips shared across all 4 subjects
  • 58 test-set clips used for ISC estimation
  • Global ISC = 0.1483 Pearson r
  • Method: Nastase et al. (2019)
